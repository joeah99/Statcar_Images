The ARCA West race kicked off the Sonoma race weekend on Friday and set the tone for what was to come: very little carnage and very few contenders. Still the race was pretty interesting in terms of who was doing the contending; the Nitro Motorsports teammates of Sam Corry and Mia Lovell. Ever since purchasing Venturini Motorsports at the end of last season, the Nitro team, which won three national ARCA Series races in 2025, had yet to find victory lane. Expanding after their purchase of Venturini to field four full-time cars in the national ARCA Series and two in the West Series, the team has been in a class of their own behind Joe Gibbs Racing and Pinnacle Racing Group on the national side, and just all over the place in the West Series.

This year, the Nitro Motorsports 25 car was supposed to be driven full-time in the West Series by rookie Julian DaCosta, but after crashing out of the first two races and showing an overall lack of speed, he was benched in place of a rotating driver lineup following the fourth race. Shockingly, DaCosta's teammate Mia Lovell, who **caused** both of DaCosta's crashes, and also showed an overall lack of speed in the first four races kept her full-time status. But credit where it's due, Lovell has been steadily improving, and was heading into Sonoma fresh off a career best third place finish at Tri-City Raceway.

Lovell, and her teammate for the race Sam Corry, were also both heading into Sonoma with crucial track experience, as both drivers competed in the Trans Am TA2 race there last year, where Corry finished fifth and Lovell finished eighth. Needless to say, Corry kept that edge over Lovell all race long in the General Tire 150, as the two Nitro Motorsports drivers ran first and second from the drop of the green flag to the checkered flag, with Corry leading all 47 laps.
